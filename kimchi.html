Kimchi

Fermened Korean-style Cabbage

Kimchi is a traditional Korean side dish of salted and fermented vegetables, such as nappa cabbage. A wide selection
of seasoning are used, including gochugaru, spring onions, garlic, ginger, and jeotgal. Kimchi is used in a variety
of soups and stews. As a staple food in Korean cuisine, it is eaten as a side dish with almost every Korean meal.

Ingredients

  • 4kg nappa cabbage
  • 16 cups of water
  • 1.5 cups of sea salt
  • 2 tbps glutinous rice flour
  • 1.5 cups of gochugaru
  • 3.5 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsps of salted fermented shrimp
  • 140g carrots
  • 1/4 cups of minced garlic
  • 2 tbsp raw sugar
    •

    Steps

    1. Cut the napa cabbage into quarters and rinse it in running water. Make sure the stem is intact.
    2. Pinch some cooking salt and rub over the thick white parts of the cabbage. Repeat this for each leaf.
    3. Put the cabbage in a large food grade plastic bag and poor saltwater over the cabbage
    4. Once the soaking process is finished, rinse the cabbages in running water to rid of the salt
    5. Mix the the rice flour, gochugaru, fish sauce, fermented shrimp, minced garlic, raw sugar, and carrots in a separate bowl.
    6. Spread this seasoning mix over each leaf of the cabbage
    7. Transfer the kimchi in an airtight container and leave it out at room temperature for 24 hours.
    8. Enjoy!
